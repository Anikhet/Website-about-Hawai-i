Oahu

Oahu is the most populous island, home to Honolulu, the state capital. Key attractions include Waikiki Beach, Pearl Harbor National Memorial, and the North Shore, famous for its surfing spots.

Oahu, also known as "The Gathering Place," is the third-largest of the Hawaiian Islands and home to the state capital, Honolulu. It is a vibrant mix of natural and cultural wonders with the modern arts, shopping, and cuisine of a bustling city.

Honolulu

Honolulu is the capital and largest city of Hawaii, known for its historic landmarks like the Iolani Palace and the iconic Waikiki Beach. It is a hub for international business and military defense, as well as a gateway to the U.S. and world.

Waikiki Beach

Waikiki Beach is one of the most famous beaches in the world, known for its golden sands, clear waters, and excellent surfing conditions. It is also a popular spot for shopping, dining, and entertainment.

Pearl Harbor

Pearl Harbor is a historic site and a reminder of the tragic events of December 7, 1941. The Pearl Harbor National Memorial includes the USS Arizona Memorial, the USS Missouri, and the Pacific Aviation Museum, offering a sobering look at history.
