History of Hawai'i

Hawaii's history is marked by the arrival of Polynesian navigators, the establishment of a complex society and monarchy, the arrival of European explorers and missionaries, the overthrow of the monarchy in 1893, and its annexation by the United States in 1898. Hawaii became the 50th state in 1959.

Geography and Climate

Geography: The Hawaiian archipelago consists of 137 islands, but the eight main islands are Hawai'i (the Big Island), Maui, O'ahu, Kaua'i, Moloka'i, Lana'i, Ni'ihau, and Kaho'olawe. The islands are volcanic in origin, with diverse landscapes ranging from lush rainforests to volcanic deserts.

Climate: Hawaii has a tropical climate with two main seasons: the dry season (May to October) and the wet season (November to April). Temperatures are generally consistent throughout the year, with coastal areas averaging 75-85°F (24-29°C).
